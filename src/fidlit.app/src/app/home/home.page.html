Mission vs. Vision Close

An organization's mission statement and a vision statement are both important components of an organization's strategy and provide direction for decision-making and goal-setting. However, they serve different purposes and focus on different aspects of the organization's future.

A mission statement defines the purpose and focus of an organization, and outlines what the company does and why it exists. It provides a framework for decision-making and guides the company's actions and priorities.

A vision statement, on the other hand, is a more aspirational statement that defines the organization's desired future state and what it wants to achieve. It is a long-term view of what the company wants to become and serves as a source of inspiration and motivation for employees.

In summary, a mission statement answers the question "What do we do?" while a vision statement answers the question "What do we want to become?"
